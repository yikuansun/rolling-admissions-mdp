\section{Setup and Assumptions}

The system operates in discrete time on a finite horizon $t \in \{1, 2, \dots, T\}$. We discretize the student population using two parameters:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Quality Tier:} $i \in \{1, \dots, r\}$, where $q_i = i/r$ represents the normalized quality of a student in tier $i$.
    \item \textbf{Attribute Combination:} $a \in \{1, \dots, A\}$, representing unique cross-products of diversity attributes (e.g., gender, nationality, socioeconomic status).
\end{enumerate}

Students are partitioned into $r \times A$ distinct buckets. To preserve high-fidelity behavioral tracking without losing computational efficiency, we track the system state using three primary multi-dimensional matrices:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Waitlist ($\mathbf{N}_t \in \mathbb{N}_0^{r \times A \times \tau_{\max}}$):} Counts of applicants who have applied but have not yet been extended an offer, partitioned by tier $i$, attribute combination $a$, and the number of periods the applicant has spent on the waitlist $\tau \in \Set{1, \dots, \tau_{\max}}$.
    \item \textbf{Matriculated Students ($\mathbf{M}_t \in \mathbb{N}_0^{r \times A}$):} Counts of students who have received an offer and accepted it, tracked by tier $i$ and attribute combination $a$.
    \item \textbf{Outstanding Offers ($\mathbf{O}_t \in \mathbb{N}_0^{r \times A \times W}$):} Counts of pending offers, partitioned by tier $i$, attribute combination $a$, and remaining decision periods $w \in \{1, \dots, W\}$.
    \item \textbf{Extension Requests ($\mathbf{R}_t \in \mathbb{N}_0^{r \times A}$):} Counts of students who reached their decision deadline ($w=1$) in the previous period and have submitted a request for a deadline extension, partitioned by tier $i$ and attribute combination $a$.
\end{itemize}

We assume the initial state $\mathbf{N}_0 = \mathbf{M}_0 = \mathbf{O}_0 = \mathbf{R}_0 = \mathbf{0}$. At any time $t$, the state of the system is uniquely described by the tuple $S_t = (\mathbf{M}_t, \mathbf{N}_t, \mathbf{O}_t, \mathbf{R}_t)$. The remaining physical capacity of the incoming class is denoted by $c_t = C - \sum_{i=1}^r \sum_{a=1}^A m_{i, a, t}$, where $C$ is the institution's total capacity target.

\section{Time-Dependent Transition Probabilities}

To align the model with historical empirical data, all transition and arrival dynamics are generalized to be time-dependent.
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Applicant Arrivals:} The total number of new arrivals at time $t$, denoted by $E_t$, follows a Poisson distribution with mean $\lambda(t)$:
\[
    E_t \sim \Poisson(\lambda(t))
\]
Each arriving applicant is independently assigned to a quality tier $i$ and attribute combination $a$ with probability $\pi_{i, a}(t)$, where $\sum_{i, a} \pi_{i, a}(t) = 1$. The number of arrivals into each specific bucket $(i, a)$ at time $t$, denoted by $\Delta n_{i, a, t}$, is jointly modeled using a multinomial distribution:
\[
(\Delta n_{1, 1, t}, \dots, \Delta n_{r, A, t}) \sim \Multinomial\left(E_t, \, [\pi_{1, 1}(t), \dots, \pi_{r, A}(t)]\right)
\]
New applicants always enter the waitlist with an initial tenure of $\tau = 1$.

    \item \textbf{Waitlist Dropouts:} A student of tier $i$ and attribute combination $a$ remaining on the waitlist with tenure $\tau$ at time $t$ independently drops out of the admissions process with a tenure-fatigued probability $\delta_{i, a}(t, \tau)$. The number of students who survive attrition and remain available for period $t+1$ is modeled binomially:
\[
    n_{i, a, \tau+1, t+1} \sim \Binomial( \tilde{n}_{i, a, \tau, t}, \, 1 - \delta_{i, a}(t, \tau) )
\]

    \item \textbf{Outstanding Offer Responses:} Let $w \in \{1, 2, \dots, W\}$ represent the number of periods remaining before an offer's decision deadline. At time $t$, a student in quality tier $i$ and attribute combination $a$ holding an offer with $w$ periods remaining will execute one of three choices:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Accept} the offer with probability $\theta_{i, a}(t, w)$,
        \item \textbf{Reject} the offer (or let it expire) with probability $\mu_{i, a}(t, w)$,
        \item \textbf{Remain undecided} with probability $1 - \theta_{i, a}(t, w) - \mu_{i, a}(t, w)$.
    \end{itemize}
    At the last period of the response window ($w = 1$), the student can no longer ``Remain undecided." Instead, we give the student the opportunity to request an extension. We let $\epsilon_{i, a}(t) = 1 - \theta_{i, a}(t, 1) - \mu_{i, a}(t, 1)$ denote the probability that the student will submit an extension request.
    
\end{itemize}

\section{Timeline and Action Constraints}

Within each period $t$, events occur in the following precise chronological order:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Arrival Phase:} The system experiences a stochastic influx of new applicants $\Delta n_{i, a, t}$ determined by the Poisson-Multinomial process. We define the intermediate, post-arrival waitlist tensor as $\mathbf{N}_t'$, where arrivals append directly to the first tenure slice:
    \[
    n_{i, a, \tau, t}' = 
    \begin{cases} 
    n_{i, a, 1, t} + \Delta n_{i, a, t} & \text{if } \tau = 1, \\
    n_{i, a, \tau, t} & \text{if } \tau > 1.
    \end{cases}
    \]
    
    \item \textbf{Decision Phase:} The school observes the post-arrival state $S_t' = (\mathbf{M}_t, \mathbf{N}_t', \mathbf{O}_t, \mathbf{R}_t)$ and selects an admission-extension pair $(\mathbf{K}_t, \mathbf{G}_t)$. The admission matrix is denoted as $\mathbf{K}_t \in \mathbb{N}_0^{r \times A \times \tau_{\max}}$, where $k_{i, a, \tau, t}$ is the number of offers extended to waitlist applicants in bucket $(i, a, \tau)$. The extension-granting matrix is denoted as $\mathbf{G}_t \in \mathbb{N}_0^{r \times A}$, where $g_{i, a, t}$ is the number of extension requests to grant to students in $\mathbf{R}_t$. Let $\mathcal{K}(S_t)$ denote the set of possible values for $\mathbf{K}_t$ and $\mathcal{G}(S_t)$ denote the set of possible values for $\mathbf{G}_t$. Due to structural and capacity constraints, we have:
    \[
    \mathcal{K}(S_t) = \Set{\mathbf{K}_t \in \mathbb{N}_0^{r \times A \times \tau_{\max}} \;\middle|\; k_{i, a, \tau, t} \leq n_{i, a, \tau, t}' \;\; \forall i, a, \tau, \text{ and } \sum_{i, a, \tau} k_{i, a, \tau, t} \leq c_t}
    \]
    \[
        \mathcal{G}(S_t) = \Set{\mathbf{G}_t \in \mathbb{N}_0^{r \times A} \; \middle| \; g_{i, a, t} \leq r_{i, a, t} \;\; \forall i, a}
    \]
    The state variables are immediately modified to their intermediate, post-decision values:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \textbf{Post-decision waitlist:} $\tilde{n}_{i, a, \tau, t} = n_{i, a, \tau, t}' - k_{i, a, \tau, t}$
        \item \textbf{Post-decision offers:} Newly extended offers, as well as granted extensions, enter at the full horizon $W$:
        \[
        \tilde{o}_{i, a, W, t} = o_{i, a, W, t} + \sum_{\tau=1}^{\tau_{\max}} k_{i, a, \tau, t} + g_{i, a, t}
        \]
        For all shorter horizons $w < W$, the outstanding offer counts remain unaltered: $\tilde{o}_{i, a, w, t} = o_{i, a, w, t}$.
        \item \textbf{Post-decision extension requests:} Since extension requests only have one period of validity, we reset after $\mathbf{R}_t$ each stage: $\tilde{\mathbf{R}}_t = \mathbf{0}$.
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{Stochastic Response Phase:} 
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Remaining waitlist students ($\tilde{\mathbf{N}}_t$) independently choose to drop out or remain based on $\delta_{i, a}(t, \tau)$.
        \item Holders of post-decision outstanding offers ($\tilde{\mathbf{O}}_t$) execute their choices to accept, reject, or remain undecided.
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{Clock Ticks:} Undecided offers age down by one period of validity, surviving waitlist candidates age by one period of tenure, and the system clock increments from $t \to t + 1$.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Stochastic Transition Dynamics}

To simulate the state's transition from time $t$ to $t + 1$, we sample from Binomial and Multinomial distributions based on the post-decision parameters.

\subsection{Waitlist Updates}
Students remaining on the post-decision waitlist experience attrition. The candidates who do not drop out advance to the next tenure increment $\tau + 1$ for the subsequent period:
\[
n_{i, a, \tau+1, t+1} \sim \Binomial(\tilde{n}_{i, a, \tau, t}, \, 1 - \delta_{i, a}(t, \tau)) \quad \forall \tau \in \{1, \dots, \tau_{\max}-1\}
\]
Note that any student reaching $\tau > \tau_{\max}$ is automatically cleared from the system due to expiration. New arrivals at $t+1$ will populate the $\tau = 1$ boundary slice.

\subsection{Outstanding Offer Decisions}
For each tier $i$, attribute combination $a$, the pool of post-decision outstanding offers $\tilde{o}_{i, a, w, t}$ splits stochastically based on the remaining validity window $w$:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Mid-Cycle Validity ($w > 1$):} The outstanding offers split into acceptances ($A_{i, a, t}^w$), rejections ($R_{i, a, t}^w$), and candidates who remain undecided ($U_{i, a, t}^w$) via a cell-wise Multinomial draw:
    \[
    \left(A_{i, a, t}^w,\ R_{i, a, t}^w,\ U_{i, a, t}^w\right) \sim \Multinomial\left(\tilde{o}_{i, a, w, t}, \ [\theta_{i, a}(t, w), \, \mu_{i, a}(t, w), \, 1 - \theta_{i, a}(t, w) - \mu_{i, a}(t, w)]\right)
    \]

    \item \textbf{Expiring Validity ($w = 1$):} Because candidates cannot remain undecided past the deadline, the pool splits into acceptances ($A_{i, a, t}^1$), outright expirations/rejections ($R_{i, a, t}^1$), and newly submitted extension requests ($E_{i, a, t}$):
    \[
    \left(A_{i, a, t}^1,\ R_{i, a, t}^1,\ E_{i, a, t}\right) \sim \Multinomial\left(\tilde{o}_{i, a, 1, t}, \ [\theta_{i, a}(t, 1), \, \mu_{i, a}(t, 1), \, \epsilon_{i, a}(t)]\right)
    \]
\end{itemize}

\subsection{State Progression to $t + 1$}
Once the stochastic variables are sampled, the components for period $t+1$ are compiled. The matriculation matrix accounts for all new acceptances across outstanding valid horizons:
\[
m_{i, a, t+1} = m_{i, a, t} + \sum_{w = 1}^W A_{i, a, t}^w \quad \forall i, \forall a
\]
The outstanding offer matrix updates by decrementing the undecided segments down by one validity horizon. For all $i \in \left\{1, \dots, r\right\}$ and $a \in \left\{1, \dots, A\right\}$, the elements progress as:
\[
o_{i, a, w, t + 1} = \begin{cases}
    U_{i, a, t}^{w + 1} & \text{if } w < W, \\ 
    0 & \text{if } w = W.
\end{cases}
\]
The extension request matrix for the next period is populated entirely by the newly generated requests emerging from the expiring $w=1$ cohort of the current period:
\[
    r_{i, a, t+1} = E_{i, a, t} \quad \forall i, \forall a
    \]
    Any un-granted requests from the current period ($\tilde{r}_{i, a, t}$) naturally drop out of the system because they do not carry forward into this transition equation.

\section{Objective Function}

The goal of the Markov Decision Process is to find an admission policy that maximizes total expected Net Benefit $Z$. The terminal reward captures cumulative class quality balanced against structural regularizations penalizing a lack of group diversity and class overbooking. 

Let $M_{a, T} = \sum_{i=1}^r m_{i, a, T}$ denote the aggregate count of matriculated students belonging to attribute combination $a$ at the terminal horizon, and let $M_T = \sum_{i=1}^r \sum_{a=1}^A m_{i, a, T}$ equal the total final class size. The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index ($\mathrm{HHI}$) measures representation concentration across diversity attribute combinations. The objective function is formulated as:
\[
    Z = \underbrace{\sum_{i=1}^r \sum_{a=1}^A m_{i, a, T} \cdot q_i}_{\text{Class Quality}} - \underbrace{\phi \cdot \sum_{a=1}^A \left( \frac{M_{a, T}}{M_T} \right)^2}_{\text{Diversity Penalty (HHI)}} - \underbrace{\psi \cdot \max\left(0, M_T - C\right)^2}_{\text{Overbooking Penalty}}
\]
where $\phi$ balances the marginal value of background representation and $\psi$ scales the quadratic cost of violating physical enrollment capacity limitations.
